HYDERABAD

Hyderabad, the capital city of Telangana in India, is a vibrant metropolis with a rich history, diverse culture, and stunning architectural landmarks. Founded in 1591 by Muhammad Quli Qutb Shah, it derived its name from his wife, Hyder Mahal. Situated on the banks of the Musi River, Hyderabad occupies the Deccan Plateau, covering an area of approximately 650 square kilometers. The city's cultural heritage is a fusion of Hindu, Muslim, and Telugu traditions, earning it the title of the "City of Pearls" due to its historical association with the pearl trade . Hyderabad's architectural wonders, such as the iconic Charminar, Golconda Fort, Qutb Shahi Tombs, and Chowmahalla Palace, stand as testaments to its glorious past. In recent years, the city has become a significant IT and BPO hub, known as "Cyberabad," hosting multinational companies, research institutions, and startup incubators. Hyderabad's cuisine, the famous Hyderabadi cuisine, is a delightful blend of Mughlai, Persian, and Telugu flavors, offering iconic dishes that tantalize the taste buds.
