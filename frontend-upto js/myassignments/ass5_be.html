BENGALURU

Bengaluru, the capital city of Karnataka in India, is a dynamic metropolis with a fascinating history and a thriving modern identity. Founded in the 16th century by Kempe Gowda I, it has grown from a small town into a bustling cosmopolitan hub. Nestled in the Deccan Plateau in southern India, Bengaluru spans an area of approximately 709 square kilometers. Renowned as the "Silicon Valley of India," the city has emerged as a global center for information technology and innovation. It houses numerous multinational IT companies, research institutions, and startup incubators, attracting talent from around the world. Bengaluru's historical significance can be seen in its architectural landmarks, such as the majestic Bangalore Palace and the ancient Bull Temple. The city's pleasant climate, vibrant nightlife, diverse culinary scene, and thriving arts and culture community contribute to its charm. Additionally, Bengaluru is known for its beautiful gardens, including Lalbagh Botanical Garden and Cubbon Park, offering respite from the bustling urban life.
