CHENNAI

Chennai, the capital city of Tamil Nadu in South India, is a bustling metropolis with a rich historical heritage and a vibrant cultural scene. Situated on the Coromandel Coast of the Bay of Bengal, Chennai spans an area of approximately 426 square kilometers. Formerly known as Madras, the city boasts a history that dates back over two millennia, witnessing the rule of dynasties such as the Cholas, Pallavas, and the British. Renowned as the "Cultural Capital of South India," Chennai is famous for its classical music, dance, and Tamil literature. The city's economy thrives on diverse industries, including automobile manufacturing and IT services. Chennai's iconic landmarks include the Marina Beach, one of the longest urban beaches in the world, the majestic Kapaleeshwarar Temple, the historical Fort St. George, and the Government Museum. With its blend of history, culture, and economic significance, Chennai captivates visitors with its unique charm and warmth.
